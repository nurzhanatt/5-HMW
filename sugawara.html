KOSHI SUGAWARA

Guy of average height and build. He has light gray (ash) hair, brown eyes with a mole under his left eye. He is usually seen with a soft smile on his face.As vice-captain, he calms his teammates and acts as a pillar of support for them. Even if he is no longer Karasuno's main setter, he doesn't think about giving up the game altogether. Thus, he encourages himself and all his teammates not to give up, no matter what difficult situation they are in. Sugawara's devotion to volleyball becomes clear when he decides to continue club activities even if most third years choose to leave to focus on their studies. Upon meeting, his teacher asked Sugawara why he chose to continue playing even if he was not a main player, and Sugawara replied that he didn't want to regret leaving in the future.

He is also quite perceptive, as he noticed Tanaka asking for the key to the gym (for Kageyama and Hinata's practice before the 3v3 game between first years), although he is always late, so Sugawara knew Tanaka was up to something. He is also smart, because he comes up with useful tactics during the game. An example of this is when he had the idea for Kageyama and Hinata's combo. Despite his nurturing demeanor, he is sometimes mischievous, as seen when he encourages Hinata to break into the youth camp uninvited.He is also prone to violence when teasing his teammates, often spanking them when he is excited to join a game, though this also has the effect of cheering the team on. He is also quite proud, although this is very rarely shown. Notable examples are when he said, "Kageyama, from now on, if you get bombarded with interviews, you'll say, 'I learned everything from the great sempai Sugawara', got it?" and when he bragged about how he came up with the name Kageyama and Hinata's new "minus tempo" technique. During the battle with Shiratorizawa, he was so annoyed by the loss of his team that he started rambling and pouting, which is very different from his usual mature demeanor.
